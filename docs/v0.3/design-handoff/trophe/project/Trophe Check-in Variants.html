The daily ritual,
reconsidered.

The habit check-in happens fourteen times per cycle, once per client per day. It's the most repeated interaction in the product. Most habit apps make it a boring checkbox tap. That's a wasted opportunity — both for the client's sense of commitment, and for the signal the coach receives.

Below are three rethought variants plus the baseline for comparison. Each tests a different hypothesis about what the moment should do.

Four approaches. Try each.

Every prototype is interactive — press, drag, swipe. The 14-dot compliance grid updates live when you commit, so you can feel what the payoff looks like.

Compare at a glance.

Friction Emotional weight Data richness Best for
Baseline Minimal None Binary Free tier, no coach
The Seal 1.2s hold High — ceremonial Binary Compliance-focused cycles; clients who need the ritual
The Dial One tap + optional note Medium 3-state + notes on partials/misses The default for paid clients. Honest data without friction on good days.
The Reflection 10-15s High — introspective Richest — mood curve + notes Premium / 1:1 coaching; clients doing deep work
Recommendation

Ship the Dial as the default. Offer the Seal and the Reflection as cycle settings the coach chooses per client.

The Dial threads the needle: it's one tap on good days, nuanced on hard days, and gives coaches the signal they need to intervene early. It converts lies-by-omission (the user tapping "done" when they half-did it) into useful data.

The Seal belongs to clients whose habit requires ceremony — recovery, sobriety, meditation. The Reflection belongs to premium tiers where the coach reads every entry. One product, three postures, selected by the person who knows the client: the coach.

Details that carry the design.

Micro

Haptic on commit.

All three variants call navigator.vibrate at the moment of commit. A short, double-tap pattern. It reinforces the psychological seal — you did something, your body knows.

Small

The 14-dot grid, live.

The day's dot scales 1.15× on commit and fills with ink. Today's dot is always the empty, bordered one — a quiet tension that resolves when you check in. The grid does the celebrating, so the button doesn't have to.

Roman

Day IX.

Roman numerals on the Seal turn a counter into a numbered chapter. Fourteen days, fourteen chapters. The user isn't tracking a streak — they're completing a volume.
