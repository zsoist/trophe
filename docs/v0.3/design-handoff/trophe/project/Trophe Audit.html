A candid read,
and a way forward.

Trophe is genuinely differentiated — the 14-day single-habit methodology is a real point of view most nutrition apps lack. But the current UI hides the moat, and the information density is fighting the product, not serving it.

Below: five findings with severity, three screens redesigned side-by-side, and three visual directions to choose from.

Findings

Severity · High
01

The methodology is invisible.

"One habit. Two weeks. Transform." is your whole pitch — but the 14-day cycle is a thin gold progress bar buried mid-dashboard. The single most differentiated thing about Trophe looks identical to every generic progress indicator.

Fix: Make the 14-dot compliance grid the hero of the client dashboard. Let the habit own the top third of the screen.

Severity · High
02

The food log is a buffet.

The tracking page imports 40+ components: three view modes, favorites chips, health-tip banner, 5 meal slots, macro rings, sugar estimator, insights, food ideas, badges, weekly summary, timeline, fasting timer, protein distribution, nutrient density, photo gallery, seven more analytics panels. Users freeze before they log.

Fix: Log view = calories + macros + meals + one CTA. Everything else lives behind "View insights" — detail on demand, not on arrival.

Severity · Medium
03

Emoji as iconography.

🔥 💪 🍽️ 🌙 🍎 appear in navigation, meal slots, stat cards and CTAs. They render differently on every OS, don't inherit text color, break with your type system, and feel consumery — which is a problem because nutritionists' livelihoods depend on this software.

Fix: Lucide is already in the stack. Use it consistently. Keep emoji, if at all, for user-authored content (meal names, coach notes).

Severity · Medium
04

Gold does too many jobs.

The gold (#D4A853) marks: brand, active nav, CTAs, progress, near-goal macros, highlights, borders, badges, streaks. When everything is accented, nothing is. The visual hierarchy collapses.

Fix: Reserve gold for one thing — the primary action the user can take right now. Everything else is type, hairline, or neutral.

Severity · Medium
05

Coach view is a dashboard, not a queue.

The coach opens the app and sees a grid of stat cards, a streak banner, a weekly summary, filter pills, and then the client list. But the coach has one real job: who do I need to talk to today? That answer is buried four screens deep.

Fix: Reframe as a queue. Top of screen: "2 clients need you today." Each flagged client is a card with context + one tap to act.

Severity · Low
06

The Greek wordmark is your strongest asset — and barely used.

τροφή is memorable, distinctive, ownable. It's currently treated like a logo-only detail. It could be a design element: drop caps on section headers, pull quotes from coach messages, a type system that feels like a book, not a SaaS app.

Fix: Lean in. The "Editorial" direction below shows one way.

Three visual directions

Use the toggle on each redesigned screen below to see all three rendered live. Each direction commits to a different aesthetic hypothesis — none is "right," but picking one is how the product finds its voice.

Direction 01

As-Is

Dark + gold + glass.

The current system, applied correctly. Commits to premium-fitness-app vocabulary (dark surfaces, gold accents, glass cards). Safe, familiar, crowded category.

Direction 02

Editorial

Paper, ink, and type doing real work.

Warm paper, deep ink, a serif that carries weight. The Greek wordmark and italic serifs feel like a well-edited book. Stands out in a category of black-and-gold lookalikes. Best fit for the brand's Mediterranean roots.

Direction 03

Precision

A data instrument.

Monochrome. Tight 8pt grid. Hairline borders. Text does the talking. Reads like a scientific tool — appropriate for coaches who sell precision nutrition and want to signal rigor over vibe.

Screen 01
Client Dashboard
The daily home screen. Current version has six cards fighting for attention before the user has done anything. Proposed version: the habit is hero, nutrition is a calm readout, the next meal is one tap away.

What changed

  • Habit is hero. 14-dot compliance grid at the top — the PN methodology made visible in the first screen.
  • One primary action. "Check in today" is a full-width button. Everything else is tertiary.
  • Nutrition is a calm readout, not a dashboard. One calorie bar, four macro columns, no rings.
  • Meals as a list with checkboxes — the mental model matches the task (is this meal done yet?).
  • Coach voice is present, not an automated "health tip." A real sentence from Michael.
  • Emoji removed in favor of minimal SVG nav.
Screen 02
Food Log
Where a 40-component kitchen sink becomes a log book. The analytics still exist — they just live behind "View insights & trends ↓", not on the primary task screen.

What changed

  • Killed the view toggle (Macros / Gauge / Radar). One canonical view for the primary task.
  • Calorie number is the hero: 44px serif. Not a ring, not a gauge.
  • Meals as a timeline with timestamps. Matches how people actually remember eating.
  • One floating CTA — "Describe what you ate." Leans on your text-AI strength.
  • Analytics moved below the fold behind an explicit affordance. Daily Insights, Weekly Summary, Timeline, Fasting Timer, Protein Distribution, Nutrient Density, Photo Gallery — all still there, not on arrival.
  • Favorites chips and health-tip banner removed from the log. They belong in a drawer, not fighting meal rows.
Screen 03
Coach Dashboard
The current version answers "how is my practice doing?" The coach's real first question is "who needs me today?" — so the redesign leads with a triaged action queue.

What changed

  • Opens with a verdict: "6 clients. 2 need you today." Not stats, a statement.
  • Action queue first: at-risk + ready-for-next clients get full cards with context + one-tap action.
  • 14-dot grids on every client card — instant read on cycle state.
  • Coach-relevant narrative ("Missed 5 of last 7 days. Mood 2.8/5.") replaces percent-complete bars.
  • Quiet roster below — on-track clients are a list, not cards. They don't need attention.
  • Killed the vanity stats (streak banner, weekly summary, KPI grid). They're an analytics page, not a home screen.

Where to go next

Shortest path

Ship the information architecture.

Keep the current dark+gold look. Move the 14-dot grid to the top, collapse the food log analytics behind a drawer, and triage the coach view into a queue. You get 70% of the benefit for ~1 week of work.

Medium

Commit to a direction.

Pick Editorial or Precision. Rebuild the type + color tokens. Replace emoji with Lucide. Re-derive the glass classes for the chosen direction. 2–3 weeks; the product starts to look unmistakable.

Deeper

Rethink the habit interaction itself.

The check-in is the product's core ritual. It deserves a deliberate animation, a haptic, a moment. I can design three variants of "the check-in moment" as a follow-up if you want.
